The original PC revolution introduced a transition from shared, centralised mainframes to individual devices of which customers had ownership. A similar transition will probably occur, where centralised LLMs will transition to local models on personal devices.  The people will thus reclaim control over their data and privacy.

A possible option is a home device with Email, Photos and Personal, sensitive details + documents stored on it. You could connect to it from your mobile device which connects to your home internet with a VPN (thus encrypted and secure) and will also be private.

On this device will be your personal LLM, which then doesn't share your data.

You thus have control over:
1. The LLM. This is beneficial because you can then have control over its capability and personality. Pricing could vary by capability of the model.
2. Your data. This may become one of the few sources of value that humans can offer economically, so it is imperative we have the option of selling it.

There are potential drawbacks with this. We are already witnessing the terrible feedback loops that these models can have, leading to harmful AI-human relationships and conversations. Therefore, this option is likely only feasible when models have reached a sufficient safety threshold.
